Bhimsen Temple

  • This gorgeous Bhimsen temple known for its fascinating three interconnected golden windows overlooking the Square was built by King Shree Nivas Malla in 1680.
  • Bhimsen, one of the five Pandavas and the great heroes of Mahabharata is known for his super strength and bravery.
  • In traditional Nepali sculptures and paintings he is always shown lifting a horse in the air and pressing an elephant under his knee while a huge cobra watches his feat in awe.
  • This image is very popular particularly in traders community of Nepal. Interestingly enough he is worshipped here more as a god of commerce and trade than in any other form.
  • The three-storey pagoda has an unusual rectangular plan that sets it apart from other temples in Patan. The current temple was completely rebuilt in 1682 after a fire and was later restored after the 1934 earthquake, and again in 1967.
  • Here non-Hindus may be able to climb to the upper level (the inner sanctum is usually upstairs in Bhimsen temples) to view the wild-eyed statue of Bhimsen.
